Responsive Redesign

For this project, I chose to redesign the website of a salon that is near where I'm from. Hoa Salon is a small business local to my home. Many people are moving to new apartment buildings in the neighborhood so I think that Hoa Salon could attract new customers with an updated website.

My redesigned version of the website can be found here.

Their original website can be accessed here.

Analysis of Original Website

Learnability:

  • The link to the directions is far down on the page and small
  • The services aren't mentioned on the homepage so it's hard to tell what they offer
  • The specials are on another page but they could be added to the home page to better advertise
  • The services are on the other page so it's not immediately obvious what they offer

Memorability:

  • The services are on a different page linked at the very bottom so you have to find that to remember what is offered

Efficiency:

  • The directions link is small and not near the listed address so it's hard to find for quick access
  • You have to click to another page to get to the specials
  • The buttons linking to other pages are at the very bottom of the page

Accesibility (assesed using WAVE tool):

  • None of the images have alternative text
  • There isn't any heading structure
  • In the opening hours, the PM is in very small text
  • There is underlined text that isn't a hyperlink

Building the Redesign

I first made some low fidelity wireframes.


Then I made a visual style guide. This served as a way for me to standardize my design and ensure that everything followed the creative vision I had.

The final step before coding was creating the high fidelity prototypes, where I was able to plan out the CSS I would need in detail.
